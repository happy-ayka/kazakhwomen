title: Historical Context
---

Born in the 1990s to a Kazakh family in Xinjiang, Ainur matured during a time when educational expansion, online culture, and gender discourse opened new spaces for self-expression, yet traditional norms remained powerful within families and ethnic communities. Her Diary of a Single Mother represents the conflict between individual autonomy and patriarchal expectations, and highlights the emotional costs of being simultaneously urban, female, minority, and single. Through her diary form and self-reflection, Ainur situates Kazakh womanhood within the moral and economic transformations of modern China.
